# Assessing a Decommissioned Landfill Using Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT)
## Executive Summary
Rapid urbanisation and population growth in Ghana have heightened the environmental risks associated with municipal solid waste disposal, especially in landfills that lack engineered containment systems. The Oti Landfill in Dompoase, which was originally located away from residential areas, now sits adjacent to urban settlements. This proximity raises concerns about leachate infiltration and gas emissions from its decommissioned cells.

This study utilises Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) to non-invasively evaluate the integrity of the capping layer and liner system within the decommissioned section of the landfill. Field surveys conducted in May 2025 aimed to determine the thickness and lateral uniformity of the cap, identify subsurface anomalies indicative of leachate and gas hotspots, and estimate the depth of the liner.

The resistivity profiles revealed zones of low resistivity (<10 Ω·m), suggesting potential leachate accumulation and compromised containment. These findings highlight the value of geophysical methods in landfill diagnostics and provide critical insights for post-closure monitoring, environmental risk mitigation, and future site redevelopment planning.

## Technical Details
This project involved the complete geophysical workflow, from raw data acquisition to inversion modeling.

* **Methodology:** Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT)
* **Array Configuration:** Schlumberger Array
* **Field Instrumentation:** AGI SuperSting™
* **Data Processing & Inversion:** AGI EarthImager™ 2D
